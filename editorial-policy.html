Editorial Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2025

Our commitment: Every piece of information published on SSCExamPrep traces back to an official, verifiable government source. We do not publish rumours, guesses, or unverified claims as facts.

📌 Our Editorial Mission

SSCExamPrep exists to be the most reliable independent aggregator of SSC exam information in India. Our editorial goal is simple: give every aspirant accurate, timely, clearly sourced information so they can act on it with confidence.

We operate independently. No government body, coaching institute, or advertiser influences what we publish or how we describe it.

🔍 Sourcing Standards

Primary Sources Only for Hard Facts

All factual updates — notifications, results, admit cards, answer keys, exam dates, vacancy counts — are sourced exclusively from:

  • ssc.gov.in — Official SSC headquarters website (primary source)
  • SSC Regional Portals — NRCGL (North), MRPQ (West), SSCNER (North East), NWRCGL (North West), SSCNR (Northern Region), SSCER (Eastern Region), SSCSR (Southern Region), SSCMPR (Madhya Pradesh & Chhattisgarh), SSCKKR (Karnataka Kerala Region)
  • Official Government Gazette — For policy-level notifications

What We Do Not Accept as Sources

  • Coaching institute websites or YouTube channels (unless they cite official sources)
  • Social media posts or WhatsApp forwards
  • "Insider" leaks or anonymous tips for factual updates
  • News aggregators that do not cite original government sources

🏷️ Labelling Unconfirmed Information

We understand aspirants sometimes need to plan ahead, even before official announcements. When we publish information that is not yet officially confirmed, we label it clearly:

  • "Expected" — Based on historical patterns or credible reports, not yet official
  • "Unofficial" — From a non-official source, published for awareness only
  • "Rumoured" — Circulating widely but not verified; published with explicit caution

We never present expected or unofficial information with the same visual treatment as confirmed facts.

✏️ Correction Policy

We take accuracy seriously. When we publish incorrect information — even if it came from an official source that was subsequently revised — we correct it as follows:

  • Immediate correction: Errors are corrected as soon as they are identified or reported
  • Transparent update: Corrected updates on the Live Updates page are marked with "(Updated)" and include the correction date
  • No silent edits: We do not silently rewrite factual errors without noting a correction
  • Acknowledgement: If you report a correction that we confirm and fix, we will acknowledge it if you wish

To report an error, use our Contact page or WhatsApp us at +91 87707 67272. We aim to investigate all correction reports within 2 hours on working days.

🚫 Editorial Independence

  • No paid placements in news updates: We do not accept payment to add, remove, or modify any update on our Live Updates page
  • No coaching institute bias: We do not favour or disfavour any coaching brand in our study material recommendations
  • No political influence: We report SSC exam data factually; we do not editorialize about government policy, hiring freezes, or vacancy cuts beyond factual reporting
  • Advertiser separation: If and when we run advertisements, they are clearly labelled and do not influence editorial content

📬 Feedback on Our Reporting

We welcome scrutiny. If you believe we have violated these editorial standards — published something inaccurate, sourced incorrectly, or failed to label unconfirmed information — please tell us.
