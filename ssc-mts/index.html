SSC MTS 2025

Multi-Tasking Staff (Non-Technical) Examination — conducted for Group C posts in Central Government offices across various ministries and departments.

📊 SSC MTS 2025 — At a Glance

Conducting BodyStaff Selection Commission (SSC)
PostMulti-Tasking Staff (MTS) & Havaldar (CBIC/CBN)
Application Last Date30 June 2025 (Extended)
Application ModeOnline — ssc.gov.in
Exam ModeComputer Based Test (CBT)
Selection ProcessCBT (Session I + II) → Physical Test (Havaldar only)
Pay Scale (MTS)Pay Level 1 (₹18,000–56,900) + Allowances
Pay Scale (Havaldar)Pay Level 2 (₹19,900–63,200) + Allowances

✅ Eligibility Criteria

🎓
Educational QualificationMatriculation (Class 10th) pass or equivalent from a recognized Board. Higher qualifications are not required.
🎂
Age LimitFor MTS: 18–25 years. For Havaldar: 18–27 years. Relaxation: SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), PwD (10/13/15 years), Ex-Servicemen as per rules.
🇮🇳
NationalityIndian citizen. Persons of specified origin from Nepal, Bhutan or other countries may also apply.
💰
Application Fee₹100/- (General/OBC). SC/ST, PwD, and female candidates are exempt from paying the fee.

📚 Exam Pattern & Syllabus

The CBT consists of two sessions conducted on the same day. Session I is for MTS and Havaldar posts; Session II is only for Havaldar post.


Session INumerical & Mathematical Ability (20Q) + Reasoning Ability & Problem Solving (20Q) = 40 Qs / 45 mins
Session IIGeneral Awareness (25Q) + English Language & Comprehension (25Q) = 50 Qs / 45 mins
LanguageSession I — Hindi & English both; Session II — English language questions in English only
Negative MarkingNo negative marking in CBT

  • Numerical Ability: Number system, decimals, fractions, LCM/HCF, ratio, percentage, simple interest, profit & loss, time-work, mensuration
  • Reasoning: Analogies, similarities, differences, space visualization, problem solving, number series, coding-decoding
  • General Awareness: India's history, geography, polity, economy, environment, science & technology, current events
  • English: Spot the error, fill in the blanks, synonyms/antonyms, idioms, one-word substitution, reading comprehension
