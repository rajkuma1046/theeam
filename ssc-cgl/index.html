🎓 SSC Exam — Graduate Level

SSC CGL 2025–26

Combined Graduate Level Examination — conducted by SSC for Group B & C posts in Central Government Ministries, Departments and Offices.

✅ Eligibility Criteria

🎓
Educational QualificationBachelor's Degree from a recognized University. Specific posts like JSO require Mathematics/Statistics at degree level.
🎂
Age Limit18–27 years (post-wise variation). Relaxation for SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), PwD (10 years), Ex-Servicemen as per government norms.
🇮🇳
NationalityIndian citizen. Subjects of Nepal/Bhutan, Tibetan refugees (arrived before 01 Jan 1962), and persons of Indian origin from specified countries are also eligible.
📝
AttemptsNo restriction on number of attempts within the age limit.

📚 Syllabus & Exam Pattern

📋 Tier I — Screening Test (CBT)

Duration: 60 minutes  |  Questions: 100  |  Max Marks: 200  |  Negative Marking: 0.50 per wrong answer


  • General Intelligence & Reasoning — 25 Questions / 50 Marks — Analogies, classification, series, coding-decoding, puzzles, blood relations, Venn diagrams, syllogism
  • General Awareness — 25 Questions / 50 Marks — History, geography, polity, economics, science, current affairs, static GK
  • Quantitative Aptitude — 25 Questions / 50 Marks — Number systems, arithmetic, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, data interpretation
  • English Language — 25 Questions / 50 Marks — Reading comprehension, cloze test, idioms, one-word substitution, error spotting, fill in the blanks
📋 Tier II — Main Examination (CBT)

Separate sessions; Computer Knowledge Test & Skill Test are qualifying in nature.


  • Paper I — Mathematical Abilities + Reasoning & GI + English (All posts)
  • Paper II — Statistics (JSO/Statistical Investigator posts)
  • Paper III — General Studies (Finance & Economics) (AAO posts)

Detailed Tier-II syllabus varies by post. Check official notification on ssc.gov.in for post-wise pattern.
